Vasco Pereira

9/15/2020

Abstract

Drivers that circulate inside cities in Portugal have a maximum speed limit of 50 km/h, but the behaviour is not always in compliance with the law. After observing the possibility of high speeds in the road Avenida Almirante Gago Coutinho in Mem-Martins, Sintra, Portugal, the vehicle speed was measured with a Raspberry Pi Zero W and an exploratory data analysis was performed in R. It was verified that the vehicles moving from Mem-Martins to Sintra had a mean speed of 69 km/h and from Sintra to Mem-Martins had a mean speed of 87 km/h This road have a pedestrian crossing about 40 meters after the experimental measuring local, and two schools and a sports hall nearby that encourage the presence of many pedestrians in the sidewalk of this road.

Conclusion

The possibility of high speeds in the road Avenida Almirante Gago Coutinho was confirmed with maximum speeds of 117 km/h which is a crime in this location.
The mean speed can be verified in the ShinyApp in both directions.

The velocity is above the permitted values - 69 km/h and 87 km/h.
It is highly recommended the imposition of preventive measures such as speed bumps.
